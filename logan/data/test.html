Question: Question: Leo's assignment was divided into three parts. He finished the first part of his assignment in 25 minutes. It took him twice as long to finish the second part. If he was able to finish his assignment in 2 hours, how many minutes did Leo finish the third part of the assignment?
Reformulated Question: Leo's assignment was divided into three parts. He finished the first part of his assignment in 25 minutes. It took him twice as long to finish the second part. If he was able to finish his assignment in 2 hours, how many minutes did Leo finish the third part of the assignment?

Answer:First, convert the total time from hours to minutes: 2 hours = 120 minutes. Leo finished the first part in 25 minutes. The second part took him twice as long as the first part, so it took 2 * 25 = 50 minutes. To find the time Leo spent on the third part, subtract the time for the first and second parts from the total time: 120 minutes - 25 minutes - 50 minutes = 45 minutes. Therefore, Leo finished the third part of his assignment in 45 minutes.

Question: Question: Jim spends 2 hours watching TV and then decides to go to bed and reads for half as long. He does this 3 times a week. How many hours does he spend on TV and reading in 4 weeks?
Reformulated Question: Jim spends 2 hours watching TV and then decides to go to bed and reads for half as long. He does this 3 times a week. How many hours does he spend on TV and reading in 4 weeks?

Answer:Each time Jim watches TV for 2 hours and reads for half as long, which is 2 hours × 1/2 = 1 hour of reading. So, each session takes 2 hours (TV) + 1 hour (reading) = 3 hours total. He does this 3 times a week, so each week he spends 3 hours/session × 3 sessions/week = 9 hours per week. Over 4 weeks, he spends 9 hours/week × 4 weeks = **36 hours** in total.
