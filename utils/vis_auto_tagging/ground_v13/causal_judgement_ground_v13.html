Causation: Even though the black wire touched the battery (as expected), the short circuit was caused because both wires touched the battery. Thus, the black wire alone didn’t cause the short circuit—it was the combination of both wires touching the battery. Conclusion: A typical person would say the black wire did not cause the short circuit alone; both wires contributed to the short circuit. The answer is {no}.
